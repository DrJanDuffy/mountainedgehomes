About Mountain's Edge

"Paradise on the outskirts of Las Vegas" - At the foothills of the Spring Mountains in the southern part of the Las Vegas Valley is Mountain's Edge. A 3,500-acre master-planned community steeped in history, today it's devoted to outdoor living with 700 acres dedicated to open green space, parks and trails.

"If you want to feel like you're on the outskirts of Las Vegas, Mountain's Edge is a good option," says Realtor Cody Corio of Knight Real Estate Group. Developed in 2004, this award-winning community is home to over 70 diverse neighborhoods with convenient access to top-notch schools, shopping centers and other amenities. With homes ranging from $380,000 to over $1 million, there's something for every style and budget.

Community Features

Parks & Recreation

Explore 700 acres of open space with Exploration Peak Park (aka "Fossil Hill"), Mountain's Edge Regional Park with its 15-26 foot climbing rings, Nathaniel Jones Park for pets, and three walking paseos throughout the community.

Community Events

Enjoy four major annual festivals including the Mountain's Edge Music Festival (May), car show (September), fall festival (October), and Winter Wonderland (December), plus family campouts, trivia nights, and artisan markets.

Top Schools

Clark County School District serves the area with Carolyn S. Reedom Elementary (B-minus rating), Doral Academy Cactus (A-minus rating), and Desert Oasis High School (B-minus rating) offering extracurricular activities and clubs.

Dining & Shopping

Visit Mountain's Edge Marketplace for Albertson's groceries and retail, enjoy table-side guacamole at El Luchador Mexican Kitchen, pizza at The Bounty, comfort food at Mountain West Eatery, or coffee at Cafe Laon.

Mountain's Edge Neighborhoods

Discover the diverse communities within Mountain's Edge, from gated luxury enclaves to family-friendly neighborhoods with unique character and amenities.

Mountain's Edge is home to over 40 distinct neighborhoods, each with its own character and charm. Many neighborhoods feature gated or guard-gated security, while others offer open community designs with easy access to parks and amenities.

The variety of builders and home designs means you'll find everything from cozy single-story homes to expansive luxury estates, with properties dating from the community's founding in 2004 to new construction opportunities still available today.
