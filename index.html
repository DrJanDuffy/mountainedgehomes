Frequently Asked Questions About Mountain's Edge Las Vegas

Find answers to the most common questions about living and buying in Mountain's Edge

What is the average home price in Mountain's Edge Las Vegas?

The average home price in Mountain's Edge is approximately $750,000, with properties ranging from $500,000 to over $1.5 million depending on size, location, and amenities. The community offers a variety of housing options across its eight distinctive neighborhoods, each with its own price range and features.

Which schools serve the Mountain's Edge community?

Mountain's Edge is served by several highly-rated schools in the Clark County School District including:

  • Elementary Schools: Wright Elementary School, Carolyn S. Reedom Elementary, and Judith D. Steele Elementary
  • Middle Schools: Lawrence & Heidi Canarelli Middle School and Faiss Middle School
  • High Schools: Sierra Vista High School and Desert Oasis High School

There are also several private and charter school options nearby including Pinecrest Academy and American Preparatory Academy.

What amenities are available in Mountain's Edge Las Vegas?

Mountain's Edge features numerous amenities including:

  • Exploration Peak Park - a 2,800-acre park with hiking trails, playground, and picnic areas
  • Nathaniel Jones Park - featuring basketball courts, splash pad, and dog park
  • Mountain's Edge Regional Park - with sports fields, walking paths, and recreation areas
  • Community centers with fitness facilities and event spaces
  • Miles of walking and biking trails throughout the community
  • Multiple neighborhood parks and green spaces
  • Nearby shopping centers, restaurants, and entertainment options
How far is Mountain's Edge from the Las Vegas Strip and airport?

Mountain's Edge is conveniently located approximately 15 miles southwest of the Las Vegas Strip, which is about a 20-25 minute drive depending on traffic. Harry Reid International Airport (formerly McCarran) is roughly 17 miles away, typically a 25-30 minute drive. The community's location offers a peaceful suburban environment while maintaining easy access to Las Vegas's world-famous attractions and transportation hubs.

What are the HOA fees in Mountain's Edge?

HOA fees in Mountain's Edge typically range from $50-150 per month depending on the specific neighborhood. These fees cover maintenance of common areas, community amenities, and neighborhood security features. Some premium neighborhoods with additional amenities may have slightly higher fees. The Mountain's Edge Master Association fee is approximately $35-50 per month, with additional sub-association fees varying by neighborhood.

Is Mountain's Edge a good investment area in Las Vegas?

Mountain's Edge has consistently shown strong property value appreciation, making it an excellent investment area in Las Vegas. The master-planned community's amenities, location, and continued development contribute to its investment potential. Since its development in the early 2000s, homes in Mountain's Edge have maintained their value well, even during market fluctuations, and have shown consistent growth over time.

What shopping and dining options are near Mountain's Edge?

Mountain's Edge residents enjoy convenient access to numerous shopping and dining options:

  • Shopping Centers: Mountain's Edge Marketplace, Blue Diamond Marketplace, and Rhodes Ranch Town Center
  • Grocery Stores: Smith's, Albertsons, Vons, and Whole Foods Market (within driving distance)
  • Dining: Various restaurants including Settebello Pizzeria, Hokkaido Teppanyaki Hibachi, PT's Gold, and numerous fast-casual options
  • Retail: Target, Home Depot, Kohl's, and other major retailers within a short drive

Additionally, the community is just a 15-20 minute drive from Downtown Summerlin, offering premium shopping, dining, and entertainment options.

What is the weather like in Mountain's Edge Las Vegas?

Mountain's Edge experiences the desert climate typical of Las Vegas with some modifications due to its slightly higher elevation:

  • Summer (June-September): Hot with temperatures frequently exceeding 100¬∞F, with low humidity
  • Winter (December-February): Mild with daytime temperatures averaging 55-65¬∞F and nights occasionally dropping to the 30s
  • Spring/Fall: Pleasant with temperatures ranging from 65-85¬∞F
  • Precipitation: Very low, with approximately 4 inches of rainfall annually
  • Sunshine: Over 300 sunny days per year

The community's elevation provides slightly cooler temperatures than the Las Vegas valley floor, offering a more comfortable environment, especially during summer months.

Is Mountain's Edge a safe neighborhood?

Mountain's Edge is considered one of the safer neighborhoods in the Las Vegas area. The community features several gated sections, active neighborhood watch programs, and regular patrols. Crime rates are lower than Las Vegas averages, and the well-planned community design contributes to its safety profile. Additionally, the Las Vegas Metropolitan Police Department has a substation relatively close to the community, ensuring quick response times when needed.

What healthcare facilities are near Mountain's Edge?

Mountain's Edge residents have access to several healthcare options:

  • Southern Hills Hospital: Full-service hospital approximately 10 minutes away
  • Spring Valley Hospital: Comprehensive medical center about 15 minutes from the community
  • Urgent Care Centers: Multiple facilities within 5-10 minutes including CareNow and UMC Quick Care
  • Medical Offices: Several medical plazas with specialists, dentists, and primary care physicians in the immediate area
  • Pharmacies: CVS, Walgreens, and Smith's Pharmacy all within the community or very close by
